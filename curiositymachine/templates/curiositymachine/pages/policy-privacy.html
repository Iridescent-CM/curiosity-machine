Iridescent is committed to protecting your privacy online. To further this commitment, we have adopted a Privacy Policy to guide how we collect, store, and use information obtained from customers who visit Curiositymachine.org. Part I of this Privacy Policy specifically pertains to information that may be collected when individuals who are ages 13 and older visit Curiositymachine.org. Part II specifically pertains to information that may be collected when children who are under age 13 visit Curiositymachine.org. The remainder, Parts III through X, pertain to all Curiositymachine.org users.


  1. Information We Collect From Individuals 13 Years Old and Older

    1. What Information Does Curiositymachine.org Collect from You?

      1. Personally Identifiable Information

        To enhance your experience on Curiositymachine.org, we collect personally identifiable information that you provide to us. Although you can visit many pages on our website without providing any personally identifiable information, you must provide certain information to take advantage of particular features, such as sharing your inventions, receiving feedback from our engineers, receiving badges, and participating in project competitions.

        Curiositymachine.org collects personally identifiable information that includes, but is not limited to:
        Name
        Address
        E-Mail Address
        Telephone Number
        Date of Birth
        Gender
        Parents' Names and E-Mail Addresses (for minors ages 13 through 17)

        We collect this personally identifiable information from you when you register on Curiositymachine.org or request products or services that Curiositymachine.org offers. We also collect this information to provide you with information about science and engineering programs that might interest you. You may be asked to provide personally identifiable information if you participate in any sweepstakes, contests, or surveys, or if you post or access any content on message boards, chat rooms, or blogs at Curiositymachine.org.

      2. Non-Personally Identifiable Information

        Curiositymachine.org also collects non-personally identifiable information automatically through the use of electronic tools such as "cookies". Cookies help provide a customized web experience by enabling us to identify web users who visit our website. You may choose not to provide such information by adjusting your Internet browser security settings to refuse cookies. Refusing cookies, however, may limit your ability to access certain pages available on Curiositymachine.org and may prevent you from enjoying the features on our website enabled by cookies.

    2. What Do We Do with Information We Collect from You?

      We may use your contact information, including your e-mail address, to communicate with you regarding products, features, and services available through Curiosity Machine or about products, features, and services that are offered by our affiliates and business partners. We may also use your contact information to send e-mail newsletters on other topics we think you might find of interest. Personally identifiable information may also be shared with marketing, advertising, or research companies.

      Personally identifiable information that Curiositymachine.org collects may also be used for other purposes, including, but not limited to, website administration and troubleshooting. Third-party service providers who provide technical support for the operation of our website and assist us in providing products and services that you have requested may access personally identifiable information for these purposes.

      Curiositymachine.org does not allow other parties to independently collect personally identifiable information about users' online activities over time and across different websites.

      Curiosity Machine may also share personally identifiable information under the following circumstances: (1) when we believe in good faith that the law requires it (and/or if compelled to do so by law enforcement or government agencies, or by other legal action); (2) when we deem it appropriate or necessary to provide such information to law enforcement authorities; (3) in connection with payments you may owe to us; (4) in the course of a sale to or merger with another business; or (5) in the event of a bankruptcy, in which case the information about the website's subscribers would be among the assets shared with or transferred to another business.

Please allow up to 10 business days to process your request. After unsubscribing, you may still receive e-mail messages about the status of your account, to resolve technical issues, or to confirm a new subscription.
