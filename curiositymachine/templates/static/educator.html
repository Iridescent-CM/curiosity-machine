Use Curiosity Machine to teach low-cost engineering design challenges in your classroom!

Curiosity Machine is a resource you can use to engage students of all ages in fun, hands-on projects that are aligned to Next Generation Science Standards.

Curiosity Machine features:

  • Free access to over 60 design challenges that will push your students to learn topics in physics, biology, engineering and more through in-depth exploration
  • Educator accounts that support you in bringing Curiosity Machine to your classroom. You can connect with your students’ accounts to easily track their progress on designs, text/video submissions, and interactions with mentors.
  • Units with lesson plans, student workbooks and NGSS standards alignment
  • Science and engineering mentors who will give your students individualized feedback on their ideas

Ready to begin?

Getting started is easy! Just follow these three steps:

  1. Click the “Get Started” button to create an Educator account.

  2. Create a group and invite your students.

    Before students under the age of 13 can join your club Curiosity Machine, their parents must fill out a consent form. If your students are over the age of 13, they can create accounts and start building.

    Once your students have accounts, invite them to your group by:

    • Entering student usernames into the recipient box. This will send an invitation to join your group.
    • Asking your students to go to their homepage and enter your class code in the “Join a Group” box. This will automatically add them to your group.

  3. Secure mentors for your students’ submissions.

    Fill out our Educator Form to let us know when your students need feedback from our scientists and engineers. It’s okay if you fill it out and things change—it just gives us an idea about how many mentors your students will need and when. It also helps us find mentors who are the right fit for your students!
